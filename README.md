## In a Nutshell (3 things to know)

1. **Food aid is being cut** – Starting April 1, 2026, about 800,000 Rohingya refugees in Bangladesh will get less food money ($7–$10/month instead of $12).
2. **Nobody has gone home** – Bangladesh shared data on 829,000 people with Myanmar, but zero repatriations have happened.
3. **Rakhine State is dangerous** – The Arakan Army controls most of the area, and human rights groups report abuses against Rohingya civilians.

---

## The Food Crisis (Most Urgent)

| Tier | Who gets this? | Monthly food money | % of people |
|------|----------------|-------------------|--------------|
| Tier 1 | Most vulnerable (disabled, elderly alone, child-headed families) | $12 | 33% |
| Tier 2 | Moderately food insecure | $10 | 50% |
| Tier 3 | Basic needs already met | $7 | 17% |

**Why this matters:** In 2023, similar cuts led to 90% of families not having enough food within 5 months, and malnutrition rates passed emergency levels.

**What refugees said:** "Food is a right, not a choice" – protests happened in late March.

## Repatriation (Going Back to Myanmar)

| Number | What it means |
|--------|----------------|
| 829,036 | Files shared with Myanmar |
| 354,751 | Verified by Myanmar |
| 253,964 | Recognized as former residents |
| **0** | **Actually returned home** |

**Bottom line:** Years of paperwork, zero movement.

---

## What's Happening in Rakhine State (Myanmar)

- Arakan Army controls **14 out of 17 townships**
- OHCHR (UN human rights office) reports: arbitrary arrests, forced labor, movement restrictions, denial of Rohingya identity
- Humanitarian access is almost completely blocked
- **Buthidaung** is approaching famine (Phase 5)
- **Maungdaw** is at critical level (Phase 4)

## Recommendations (What Needs to Happen)

- **Donors:** Give more money to WFP food programs now
- **ASEAN countries:** Create a rescue plan for refugees at sea
- **Bangladesh:** Set up an appeals system for families who disagree with their food tier
- **Arakan Army:** Stop blocking aid, end forced labor, allow Rohingya to move freely
